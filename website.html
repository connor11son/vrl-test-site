Vision Research Lab

At the Vision Research Lab (VRL) at UC Santa Barbara, we conduct research in various areas of computer vision, machine learning, and image processing.

Our focus is on developing innovative algorithms and technologies that can be applied to solve real-world problems in areas such as medical imaging, biological image analysis, remote sensing, and more. Led by faculty members with expertise in computer vision, machine learning, and related fields, our lab provides a collaborative environment for researchers at all levels to explore new ideas and contribute to the advancement of computer vision technologies.

Research Areas

Our lab specializes in several key areas of computer vision and machine learning research.

Deep Learning for Visual Recognition

About BisQue

A comprehensive platform for biological image analysis and management.

BisQue (Bio-Image Semantic Query User Environment) is an open-source platform developed at the Vision Research Lab, UCSB, for the management, analysis, and sharing of biological images. It is designed to address the challenges of working with complex, multi-dimensional scientific images in a collaborative research environment.

BisQue provides researchers with tools to:

  • Store and organize large collections of images with associated metadata
  • Perform image analysis using a variety of built-in and custom algorithms
  • Share images and analysis results with collaborators
  • Integrate with other research tools and workflows

The platform supports a wide range of image formats and can handle multi-dimensional data (2D, 3D, 4D, and beyond), making it suitable for various scientific applications, including microscopy, medical imaging, and remote sensing.

Key Features

BisQue offers a comprehensive set of features for scientific image analysis and management.

Image Management

Store, organize, and search large collections of scientific images with customizable metadata schemas.

Image Analysis

Access a library of image analysis algorithms or develop custom analysis workflows using Python, MATLAB, or other tools.

Collaboration

Share images, annotations, and analysis results with collaborators and control access permissions.

Visualization

View and interact with multi-dimensional images using advanced visualization tools, including 3D rendering.

Use Cases

BisQue is used in various scientific domains for image-based research.

Marine Science

BisQue is used by marine scientists to analyze images of coral reefs, track changes over time, and study marine ecosystems. The platform's ability to handle large image datasets and support collaborative research makes it valuable for multi-institutional projects.

Plant Biology

In plant biology, BisQue helps researchers analyze images of plant structures, measure growth patterns, and study phenotypic traits. The platform's image analysis capabilities enable automated processing of large numbers of plant images for high-throughput studies.

Medical Research

Medical researchers use BisQue to manage and analyze medical images, such as MRI scans, microscopy images of tissue samples, and other biomedical data. The platform's support for metadata and annotation helps in organizing complex datasets and facilitating collaboration among research teams.

Research

Our lab focuses on developing innovative algorithms and technologies in computer vision, machine learning, and image processing.

Research Areas

At the Vision Research Lab, we focus on developing innovative algorithms and technologies in computer vision, machine learning, and image processing. Our research spans a wide range of areas, from fundamental theoretical work to applied research with real-world applications.

Biomedical Image Analysis

Our lab develops computer vision and machine learning methods for analyzing biomedical images, including microscopy, histology, and medical imaging. We work on problems such as cell detection and segmentation, tissue classification, and disease diagnosis, collaborating with biologists and medical researchers to address real-world challenges in biomedical research and clinical practice.

Key projects include automated analysis of histopathology images for cancer diagnosis, deep learning for medical image segmentation, and the development of the BisQue platform for bioimage management and analysis.

3D Computer Vision

We work on various aspects of 3D computer vision, including 3D reconstruction from images, point cloud processing, and object detection and recognition in 3D data. Our research in this area has applications in robotics, autonomous driving, augmented reality, and environmental monitoring.

Current projects focus on 3D object detection using transformer networks, self-supervised learning for depth estimation, and efficient algorithms for processing large-scale point clouds.

Deep Learning for Visual Recognition

Our lab develops deep learning methods for various visual recognition tasks, including object detection, image classification, semantic segmentation, and action recognition. We work on both fundamental research to improve the accuracy, efficiency, and robustness of deep learning models, as well as applied research in specific domains.

Recent projects include self-supervised learning for representation learning, efficient neural network architectures for edge devices, and domain adaptation for visual recognition in challenging environments.

Environmental Monitoring

We apply computer vision and machine learning techniques to environmental monitoring, including the analysis of satellite and aerial imagery, underwater imagery, and ground-based sensors. Our work in this area contributes to understanding and addressing environmental challenges, such as climate change, biodiversity loss, and ecosystem health.

Ongoing projects include automated coral reef monitoring using deep learning, change detection in satellite imagery for deforestation tracking, and computer vision for biodiversity monitoring.
